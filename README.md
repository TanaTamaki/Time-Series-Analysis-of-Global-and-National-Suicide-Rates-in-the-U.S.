# Time_Series_Analysis_Global_and_National_Suicide_Rates
WHO suicide data from 109 countries spanning three decades. National and subgroup analysis of suicides in the United States.


## Overview
Suicide remains a major global public-health concern consuming the lives of more than 700,000 people each year. To improve current prevention policies, it is vital to understand how demographic patterns have changed over time and which groups face the greatest risk of suicide. 

## Objectives
- Analyze suicide rates to identify demographic patterns across age, sex, and generations. 
- Combine global context of suicide trends with detailed national and subgroup analysis inthe United States.
- Provide  data-driven understanding of shifting trends in suicide rates to create a clear picture of where suicide risk is concentrated and how it may change in the future.
